Inverse Trigonometry

Formula

dy/dx [(f(x))n] = n(f(x))n-1 × f′(x)

Example

Find the derivative of y = (4x-8)2

Step 1

First, multiply the function by the exponent 2 and subtract the current exponent by 1 to get:

2(4x-8)2-1

Step 2

Once you do this, then we have to multiply the function by the derivative of the function within the parentheses:

2(4x-8)2-1×(4)

Step 3

Simplify the problem by multiplying the first function by 2:

(8x-16)×(4)

Step 4

Finally, multiply both functions to get your final answer:

32x-64
