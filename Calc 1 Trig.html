Formulas

dy/dx (sin(x)) = cos(x)   dy/dx (cos(x)) = -sin(x)
dy/dx (tan(x)) = sec2(x)   dy/dx (csc(x)) = -csc(x)cot(x)
dy/dx (sec(x)) = sec(x)tan(x)   dy/dx (cot(x)) = -csc2(x)

Example 1

Find the derivative of y = sin(3x+5)

Step 1

First, take the derivative of the trig identity:

cos(3x+5)

Step 2

Next, multiply the identity by the derivative of the function in the parentheses. This is because the chain rule forces us to take the derivative of both functions and multiply them:

cos(3x+5) × (3)

Step 3

Once you do this, simplify to get your final answer:

3cos(3x+5)

Example 2

Find the derivative of y = csc(x2)

Step 1

First, take the derivative of the trig identity:

-csc(x2)cot(x2)

Step 2

Next, multiply the identity by the derivative of the function in the parentheses. This is because the chain rule forces us to take the derivative of both functions and multiply them:

-csc(x2)cot(x2) × (2x)

Step 3

Once you do this, simplify to get your final answer:

-2xcsc(x2)cot(x2)
